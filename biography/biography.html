Behind the Screen

A Journey Built by Code and Circuits

Childhood Years

1My childhood was deeply influenced by the world of computers and technology. 2My father owned a small computer shop, which became my second home. 3While other children played outside, I spent my time watching computer parts being assembled. 4I was fascinated by the green motherboards and tiny electronic components. 5Every day felt like a new lesson inside that shop. 6I learned early that computers were not just machines but systems that required harmony. 7My father taught me patience and discipline when handling hardware. 8I still remember holding a processor carefully, afraid of bending its pins. 9This moment sparked my curiosity about how technology worked. 10I began asking questions about RAM, hard drives, and power supplies. 11Watching was no longer enough for me. 12I wanted to participate and build something myself. 13My father guided me step by step as I installed components. 14The sound of a component clicking into place excited me. 15Each successful build gave me confidence. 16I learned the importance of compatibility and organization. 17Cable management taught me discipline and cleanliness. 18Technology stopped being mysterious and became familiar. 19I felt proud when friends asked for help fixing their computers. 20Those childhood experiences shaped my love for technology.

Teenage Years

1As I entered my teenage years, my hobby evolved into a serious skill. 2In high school, my teachers noticed my strong hardware knowledge. 3I was chosen to represent my school in a PC assembly competition. 4The pressure was intense, but I trusted my experience. 5I practiced assembling computers repeatedly at home. 6Speed and accuracy became my focus. 7On competition day, I relied on muscle memory. 8My hands moved confidently, just like my father taught me. 9I placed second place, which filled me with pride. 10That achievement motivated me to aim higher. 11In senior high school, I joined a robotics project using Arduino. 12I worked on motors, sensors, and wiring. 13I realized hardware needed software to function properly. 14This realization pushed me to learn programming. 15I enjoyed writing logic that controlled machines. 16Our robot project won Best Thesis Group Project. 17This proved my ability in both hardware and coding. 18I competed against other skilled students from different schools. 19These experiences broadened my perspective on technology. 20My teenage years connected my past to my future.

College Years

1Entering college forced me to make an important decision. 2Many expected me to take BSIT because of my hardware skills. 3Instead, I chose Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. 4I wanted to understand how computers think and process data. 5Building hardware alone was no longer enough for me. 6Computer science challenged my problem-solving abilities. 7I learned programming languages and algorithms. 8Abstract thinking became part of my daily routine. 9My hardware background gave me an advantage. 10I understood how software interacts with physical machines. 11This dual skill set strengthened my confidence. 12I continued to fix computers while learning to code. 13Debugging programs felt similar to troubleshooting hardware. 14I realized my past experiences still mattered. 15I consider hardware knowledge a valuable asset. 16My goal is to work in a leading tech company. 17I also dream of building my own technology business. 18College helped me discover my true potential. 19I now see technology as both art and science. 20This journey excites me as I move toward the future.
